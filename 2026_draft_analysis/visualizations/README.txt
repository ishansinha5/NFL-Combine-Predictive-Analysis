# Understanding the Visualizations

This folder contains the visual outputs generated by the `team_grader.py` and `position_grader.py` scripts. 

When dealing with historical NFL data, time is a massive variable. A player drafted in 2021 has had four full years to accumulate stats, while a player drafted in 2025 has only had one. To account for this, the pipeline is capable of outputting graphs based on two entirely different mathematical targets. 

Here is an atomic breakdown of how to read the charts in the respective subfolders:

### 1. The Normalized Folder (Default)
**What it measures:** Average wAV (Weighted Approximate Value) Per Season. 
**Why we use it:** This is the superior metric for evaluating a new rookie class. It takes a historical player's total career value and divides it by the years they have played. This measures pure *impact per season*. It levels the playing field so a superstar second-year player isn't penalized by the algorithm simply for being young compared to a five-year veteran. 
**How to interpret:** If a rookie has a high Normalized score, the models believe their specific combination of athletic traits historically translates to an immediate, high-impact presence on the field.

### 2. The Cumulative Folder
**What it measures:** Total Career wAV.
**Why we use it:** This measures absolute longevity and total career production. It is heavily biased towards players who have survived in the league for a long time. 
**How to interpret:** If a rookie has a high Cumulative score but a lower Normalized score, the models suggest their athletic profile points toward a long, steady career as a reliable starter or depth piece, rather than an explosive immediate superstar.

### 3. Reading the Grouped Bar Charts
Inside the team and position subfolders, you will find grouped bar charts for specific rosters and position groups. 
* **The X-Axis:** The drafted players, sorted from highest overall average score to lowest.
* **The Y-Axis:** The predicted wAV value.
* **The Bars:** Every player has two bars. The first bar is the **SVM Model Prediction** (Support Vector Machine). The second bar is the **Random Forest Prediction**. 

Looking at both bars allows you to instantly see model consensus. If both bars are high, both the linear and non-linear mathematical engines agree that the player is a stud. If the bars wildly diverge, it means the player has a highly unconventional athletic profile that the models don't quite know how to agree on.
